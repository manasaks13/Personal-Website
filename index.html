About Me!

Hello! I’m Manasa K Sheshadri!

Hi, I’m Manasa, a UI/UX enthusiast and creative thinker from Bangalore who loves turning ideas into clean, meaningful designs. I’ve always been drawn to the blend of creativity and logic — whether I’m crafting intuitive interfaces, brainstorming design flows, or simply observing how people interact with the digital world. I completed my schooling at MES Kishore Kendra Public School, followed by my PUC at BASE PU College, and I’m currently pursuing my B.E. in Computer Science Engineering at Sir M. Visvesvaraya Institute of Technology. Along the way, I discovered that design is not just something I enjoy — it’s my passion and the direction I want my future to grow in. When I’m not designing or learning new creative tools, you’ll find me on the badminton court, warming up my competitive side, or solving strategic battles over a game of chess. I love designing, learning, exploring, and building experiences that feel simple, modern, and delightful.

Floppy Bird

**Flappy Bird** is a popular 2D arcade-style game where the player controls a small bird that must fly through narrow gaps between vertically placed pipes. By tapping the screen (or pressing a key), the bird flaps its wings and moves upward, and the player must avoid hitting the pipes or the ground. The game is known for its simple controls, challenging gameplay, and highly addictive nature.

A sunrise and sunset animation visually captures the movement of the sun as it rises and sets, transitioning through warm and soothing colors. It usually begins with soft morning shades, brightens as the sun climbs higher, and ends with rich evening tones as the sun dips below the horizon. This animation creates a peaceful and natural atmosphere, often used to represent the passage of time or add a calming visual effect.
